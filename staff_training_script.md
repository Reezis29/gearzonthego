# Gearz On The Go: Staff Training Script

**Version 1.0 | Last Updated: 7 March 2026**

---

## Introduction

Welcome to the Gearz On The Go team! This document is your guide to handling camera pickups and returns professionally and efficiently. Following these steps ensures a smooth experience for our customers and protects our equipment.

This script is based on our official **Staff Operations Flowchart**. Please keep a copy of the flowchart handy for quick reference.

## Part 1: The Pickup Process

This is the customer's first physical interaction with us. The goal is to be friendly, fast, and thorough.

### Step 1: Greet the Customer

When a customer enters, greet them warmly.

> **You:** "Hi, welcome to Gearz On The Go! Are you here for a pickup or a return?"

If they say **Pickup**, proceed to the next step.

### Step 2: Find the Booking

Retrieve the customer's booking in the system.

> **You:** "Great! Do you have your booking reference number? It would be in the confirmation email we sent."

| Customer Response | Your Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Gives Booking Ref** | Search by the reference number in the system. This is the fastest way. |
| **Doesn't have Ref** | "No problem at all. Can I get the full name or phone number you used to make the booking?" Then, search by that information. |

### Step 3: Verify Booking & Identity

Once you find the booking, confirm the details and verify the customer's identity.

> **You:** "Okay, I see you have a booking for the **[Camera Name]** for **[Number]** days. Is that correct?"

After they confirm:

> **You:** "Perfect. For security, may I please see your IC or Passport for verification?"

| Scenario | Your Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ID Matches Booking** | Proceed to the next step. |
| **ID Does NOT Match** | **Do not proceed.** Explain calmly: "I'm sorry, but for security reasons, the name on the ID must match the name on the booking. We can only hand over the equipment to the person who made the reservation. Is [Name on Booking] here with you?" If they are not, you cannot release the camera. Contact the manager if the customer insists. |
| **Booking Status is NOT 'Confirmed'** | If the status is 'Pending' or 'Cancelled', inform the customer: "It looks like the booking fee for this reservation hasn't been paid, so it wasn't confirmed. You can make a new booking now if you'd like." |

### Step 4: Collect Payment

Clearly state the amount due.

> **You:** "Alright, everything looks good. The total amount due for pickup today is **RM[Amount]**. That consists of the remaining rental fee plus the **RM200 fully refundable security deposit**."

- Collect the payment via cash or card.
- Update the payment status in the system.

### Step 5: Sign the Agreement

> **You:** "We just need you to sign our digital rental agreement. Please take a moment to read through the terms and conditions here on the tablet."

- Ensure the customer signs in the designated area.
- Save the signed agreement in the system.

### Step 6: Prepare & Hand Over the Camera

While the customer is signing, prepare the camera. Use this checklist every time.

**Camera Prep Checklist:**
- [ ] **Visual Inspection:** Check the camera body and lens for any existing scratches or damage. Make sure it matches the pre-rental condition notes.
- [ ] **Functionality Check:** Turn the camera on.
- [ ] **Format Card:** Go to settings and format the memory card to ensure it's empty and ready for the customer.
- [ ] **Battery Check:** Confirm the battery is at or near 100%.
- [ ] **Bag Check:** Ensure all standard accessories (e.g., charger, extra battery, bag) are present.

Once ready, hand the equipment to the customer.

> **You:** "Here is your **[Camera Name]**. I've just formatted the memory card and the battery is fully charged. The bag contains the charger and an extra battery. Do you have any questions about using it?"

### Step 7: Finalize in System

This is the most important final step.

> **You:** "Okay, you're all set! I'm just marking this booking as **active** in our system now."

- Click the **"Mark as Active"** button.
- This action records the official pickup time and starts the rental period.

> **You:** "Enjoy your trip and happy shooting! We'll see you back here on **[Return Date]**."

## Part 2: The Return Process

The goal here is to check the equipment thoroughly and process the deposit refund efficiently.

### Step 1: Greet & Find Booking

> **You:** "Welcome back! Are you here to return a camera?"
> 
> **You:** "Great. Can I get your name or booking reference, please?"

- Find the booking in the system.

### Step 2: Inspect the Equipment

This is the most critical step of the return process.

> **You:** "Thank you. Let me just inspect the equipment. It will only take a couple of minutes."

**Camera Inspection Checklist:**
- [ ] **Physical Condition:** Carefully check the camera body, screen, and lens for any new scratches, dents, or cracks. Pay close attention to the lens glass.
- [ ] **Accessories:** Check the bag to ensure all items (extra battery, charger, cables, lens cap) have been returned.
- [ ] **Functionality:** Turn the camera on to ensure it powers up correctly.
- [ ] **Memory Card:** Confirm the memory card is still in the camera.

### Step 3: Process the Refund

| Inspection Result | Your Action & Dialogue |
| :--- | :--- |
| **No Damage, All Items Returned** | This is the best-case scenario. "Everything looks perfect, thank you! I'm processing your **full RM200 deposit refund** now. You should see it back in your account within [X] business days." |
| **Minor Damage or Missing Item** | **Be calm and professional.** "I've noticed a [describe the issue, e.g., 'new scratch on the screen' or 'the charger seems to be missing']. As per the rental agreement, we'll need to deduct a small amount from the deposit to cover this. Let me show you." <br> - Take clear photos of the damage and upload them to the booking record in the system. <br> - Refer to the company's damage fee list to calculate the deduction. <br> - Inform the customer of the exact amount being deducted and the balance being refunded. |
| **Major Damage** | "I'm sorry, but it looks like the camera has sustained some significant damage here. I'll need to get my manager to assess this before we can process the deposit." <br> - **Do not argue with the customer.** <br> - Document everything with photos. <br> - Contact the manager immediately. |

### Step 4: Finalize in System

After processing the refund, finalize the booking.

> **You:** "Okay, I've processed your refund and I'm now marking the booking as **Returned** in our system."

- Click the **"Mark as Returned"** button.

> **You:** "Thank you for renting with Gearz On The Go. We hope you got some amazing shots! Have a great day."
